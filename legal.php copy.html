Before using any product, review the instructions carefully, and if any doubts or questions remain, consult local professionals or authorities. What follows are reminders, although not an exhaustive list, of safety measures you should be aware of when using our NanodotsTM magnetic micro constructors. They are not substitutes for your own caution, care, good judgment and common sense.

General Safety Instructions:

Proper use

Although Nanodots are coated with several layers of metallic surface, they can still be damaged if excessive force is applied. Do not compress or exert excessive pressure on the magnets in order to prevent cracking or deformation. Avoid contact between the magnets and other hard metallic surfaces. Do not throw or snap together the magnets. Do not cut or grind the magnets as the magnetic material can ignite. To separate magnets, carefully slide them apart instead of attempting to pull them apart. Magnets can demagnetize other electronic products when placed in close proximity. Keep magnets away more than 15cm (6 inches) away from credit cards, software, or any other magnetic storage media. Do not drop the magnets. If dropped, make sure that no individual magnets have detached. Keep out of reach of children at all times.

Contact with body

If using our magnets for medical purposes and health benefits, please consult your doctor. People with pacemakers, insulin pumps and other medical implants or devices should consult their doctor before using or handling our magnets. Certain metal alloys can cause an allergic reaction from coming in contact with skin and especially internal organs. Never put magnets in your mouth and avoid contact with sensitive body parts.

WARNING! Magnets sticking together or becoming attached to a metallic object inside the human body can cause serious or fatal injury â€“ seek immediate medical help if magnets are swallowed or inhaled. Our magnets should not be used by children below 6 years old to avoid risk of accidental swallowing or injury.

If any signs of irritation or allergic reaction appear from ordinary contact with the magnets, discontinue using the product. If symptoms continue, immediately contact a doctor or physician.

Proper storage

Nanodots magnets can be stored together without losing magnetic field intensity. If several magnets are being stored, they should be stored in attracting positions. Do not expose magnets to high temperatures - they may lose their magnetic properties in temperatures above 100Â°C (212Â°F).

Due to strong magnetic fields, Nanodots can attract metallic dust and debris, which may be difficult or impossible to remove. Store magnets in closed containers, so that they don't attract metal debris. Do not put magnets in places where metallic dust is apparent or may arise, such as workshops, garages, tool boxes, etc.

Proper disposal

Occasionally it may be necessary for you to dispose of magnets. Please observe the following so that you protect the environment, and do not violate local, state or federal laws. If the magnets are magnetized, and you have the means of thermally demagnetizing them (by taking the magnet to its curie temperature) in a safe environment, then you should try and do this. If such means are not available, make sure to use some type of shielding (galvanized steel sheets work great) to line the boxes or containers that will be used. This will prevent the magnets from sticking to the disposal containers.
